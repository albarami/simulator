## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Overview
This report analyzes the Ministry of Labour's service fee structure across 54 services processing 3.25 million annual requests. Our analysis reveals **83.7 million QAR in documented, untapped revenue opportunities** from 18 services where operational teams have provided specific fee recommendations grounded in market understanding and operational feasibility.

### Key Findings

**Current State:**
- **54 services** across 9 categories with 3.25M annual requests
- **Current revenue:** 67.0M QAR (91% from single service)
- **36 services (67%)** operate at zero fees
- **18 services (33%)** have documented operational fee suggestions

**Revenue Opportunity:**
- **Immediate potential:** 83.7M QAR annually from documented suggestions
- **Quick wins:** 64.3M QAR from 14 high-confidence suggestions
- **Top 4 services alone:** 53M QAR potential

### Strategic Recommendation

**Implement a phased fee structure over 12 months targeting 50-60M QAR revenue increase** through:

1. **Phase 1 (Months 1-4):** Employer-paid conditional fees - 40M QAR potential, <5% demand risk
2. **Phase 2 (Months 5-8):** Recurring and per-unit fees - 22M QAR potential, <8% demand risk  
3. **Phase 3 (Months 9-12):** Worker-paid fees with waivers - 10M QAR potential, requires careful messaging

**Conservative projection with 10% demand elasticity adjustment: 47M QAR net revenue increase (70% growth)**

- **Demand Elasticity:** 10-15% (workers are price-sensitive)
- **Political Sensitivity:** **HIGH** - Civil society monitoring, potential media scrutiny
- **Reputational Risk:** Could be framed as "commodifying worker rights"

**Risk Mitigation Strategies:**

1. **Messaging is Critical:**
   - Frame as "administrative processing fee" (ÿ±ÿ≥ŸàŸÖ ÿ•ÿØÿßÿ±Ÿäÿ©) equivalent to employer-side costs
   - NOT a "permission fee" or "transfer fee"
   - Emphasize parity principle (same 100 QAR whether employer or worker initiates)

2. **Social Safety Net:**
   - Offer fee waivers for vulnerable workers (monthly salary <1,500 QAR)
   - Waiver application process must be simple and non-stigmatizing
   - Estimated waiver impact: 10-15% of volume, reduces revenue by 900K-1.35M QAR

3. **Timing:**
   - Implement ONLY AFTER Phase 1 and 2 fees are normalized
   - Workers will be more accepting if employers are also paying fees

## SECTION 5: RISK ASSESSMENT & MITIGATION

### 5.1 Demand Elasticity Risks

| Risk Level | Services | Mitigation Strategy |
|------------|----------|---------------------|
| **Low (<5% drop)** | Employer-paid, legal compliance services | Launch with minimal concern; monitor first 90 days |
| **Medium (5-10% drop)** | Recurring fees, optional services | Pilot test; offer early-bird discounts; monitor monthly |
| **High (10-15% drop)** | Worker-paid services | Phased approach; waiver program; strong messaging campaign |

**Overall Portfolio Risk:** Low - Weighted average demand drop estimated at **7%** across all implemented fees.

### 5.2 Political & Reputational Risks

**High-Risk Scenarios:**

1. **"Ministry Profiting from Workers" Narrative**
   - **Risk:** Media/civil society frame worker-paid fees as exploitative
   - **Likelihood:** Medium (if poorly messaged)
   - **Impact:** High (reputational damage, potential policy reversal)
   - **Mitigation:** 
     - Implement worker-paid fees LAST (Phase 3)
     - Robust waiver program for low-income workers
     - Emphasize administrative processing cost recovery
     - Highlight continued free services (36 services remain free)

2. **"Double-Charging" Perception**
   - **Risk:** Employers complain about multiple fees for same hiring process
   - **Likelihood:** Low-Medium
   - **Impact:** Medium (lobbying for fee rollback)
   - **Mitigation:**
     - Transparent fee schedule published upfront
     - Bundle related fees where possible
     - Emphasize per-transaction fairness (pay for what you use)

3. **"Government Revenue Grab" Criticism**
   - **Risk:** Perception that fees are revenue-driven, not cost-recovery
   - **Likelihood:** Medium
   - **Impact:** Medium (public trust erosion)
   - **Mitigation:**
     - Commission independent cost-of-service study
     - Demonstrate fee levels below cost-recovery in many cases
     - Publish revenue utilization plan (digital transformation, service improvements)

### 5.3 Operational Risks

**System Readiness:**
- **Challenge:** Monthly recurring billing not currently supported
- **Impact:** Phase 2 delayed or loan worker fees implemented as annual instead
- **Mitigation:** IT assessment in Month 1; if monthly billing not feasible by Month 5, implement as quarterly or annual with adjusted rates

**Data Gaps:**
- **Challenge:** Missing average duration data for loan worker authorizations
- **Impact:** Revenue projections may be significantly off
- **Mitigation:** Immediate data request to operations team; use conservative 1-month average until verified

**Conditional Fee Complexity:**
- **Challenge:** Determining employer entity type (government vs private) adds processing step
- **Impact:** May slow transaction processing
- **Mitigation:** Employer self-declaration with spot audits; integrate with existing company registry data
